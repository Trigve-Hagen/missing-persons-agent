Executive Summary

This proposal seeks $[Amount] in federal funding to transition the open-source repository missing-persons-agent from a prototype into an enterprise-grade, law enforcement-ready AI platform. The system leverages Autonomous LLM Agents to automate the gathering of Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) and parse public tips during missing person investigations.

By automating manual data aggregation during the critical first 48 hours of a disappearance, this software drastically reduces investigative bottlenecks, allowing detectives to focus purely on high-value leads and field work.

Project Overview & Problem Statement

Every year, hundreds of thousands of missing person cases overwhelm local police agencies. Investigative success is directly tied to response velocity. However, detectives are plagued by operational bottlenecks:

  • Data Overload: Sifting through thousands of public tips, social media footprints, and neighborhood forum posts manually is incredibly slow.
  • Fragmented Intelligence: Cross-referencing disparate databases, geolocations, and physical descriptions frequently leads to siloed data.

The open-source missing-persons-agent architecture solves this by deploying a collaborative web of specialized AI agents. They automatically scan open web networks, organize timelines, and filter high-priority tips, bringing immediate, actionable intelligence to the primary investigator.

Project Scope & Key Deliverables

Funding will be utilized over an 18-month cycle to transform the codebase across three primary pillars:

  1. Security & Compliance Upgrades
    • CJIS Compliance: Re-architecting the application layer to meet strict Criminal Justice Information Services (CJIS) data security requirements.
    • On-Premise / GovCloud Deployment: Creating standardized containerized deployments (Docker/Kubernetes) optimized for secure AWS GovCloud or local precinct servers.
  2. Feature Implementation & System Hardening
    • Advanced OSINT Scraping: Building secure connectors for localized news networks, public geodata, and social media platforms.
    • Human-in-the-Loop Safeguards: Creating a strict UI review system ensuring AI recommendations must be formally verified by a sworn detective before case file integration.
    • Interoperability Engines: Developing APIs to seamlessly push parsed data directly into standard Records Management Systems (RMS) used by law enforcement.
  3. Pilot Program & Field Testing
    • Deploying the hardened software across [Number] partner agency precincts for a 6-month trial phase.
    • Benchmarking case-clearance times against historical averages to quantify efficacy.
